About Redwood Horizon

Rooted in care. Built around people.

Our Mission

Redwood Horizon provides whole-person, community-based wraparound services that help people facing barriers to stability stay connected to care and community, and move toward self-determination.

Redwood Horizon stays with people through complexity.

Our vision is a Northern California where no one is left to navigate fragmented systems alone — where every person has access to care that honors their full life, not just a single diagnosis or program category.

Why "Redwood Horizon"? The redwood stands for rootedness, resilience, and quiet strength — the same qualities we bring to every relationship. The horizon is what lies ahead: possibility, a broader view, a future that isn't determined by the past. Together, they reflect who we are — grounded in where we come from and committed to what people can become.

Our Pillars

Six principles that guide everything we do

Whole-Person Support

We coordinate across health, behavioral health, housing, benefits, employment, and re-entry — starting with what matters most to each person, not what fits a checkbox.

Community-Based Care

Services happen where people live and need them most. We come to you — in your home, your community, or wherever is safest and most comfortable.

Persistent Engagement

We don't disengage when things get complicated. When one door closes, we find another way. We stay with people through complexity, on their timeline.

Systems Navigation

Fragmented systems shouldn't fall on the people who need them most. We bridge across health plans, county agencies, courts, and providers so people don't have to.

Person-Paced Partnership

Readiness and trust build on the timeline of the people we serve — not ours. We meet people where they are and move at the pace that works for them.

Evidence-Informed Practice

Our approach draws from proven models for care coordination, behavioral health, and community-based support — adapted to the real conditions of Northern California.

Our Approach

What "Wraparound" Means

Most services are designed around a single problem — housing, or mental health, or employment. Wraparound is different. It starts with the whole person and coordinates across everything that affects their stability. That means housing, yes — but also health care, behavioral health, benefits, re-entry support, employment, and the daily logistics that often fall through the cracks.

How We Work

  • We come to you — services are delivered in the community, not just in offices
  • We stay with you — we don't disengage when things get complicated
  • We work at the pace of the people we serve — readiness and trust build on your timeline, not ours
  • We coordinate across systems — you shouldn't have to navigate fragmented services alone

Evidence-informed models. Our approach draws from evidence-based models for care coordination, behavioral health support, supported employment, and working with people involved in the justice system. We adapt these models to the real conditions of Northern California — the distances, the limited resources, the communities and cultures we serve.
